    Our classes from Basic Stage (B.S.) 7 to 9, having a class size of thirty (30), are well- spaced, comfortable and bright. Our professional tutors use the Standard Based Curriculum from the National Council for Curriculum Assessment (NACCA) in their delivery.


    Subjects offered include English Language, Mathematics, Integrated Science, and Social Studies, which make up the Core Subjects. Ghanaian Language (Asante Twi), Religious and Moral Education (R.M.E.), Basic Design and Technology (B.D.T.), French and Computing are the Elective Subjects available. However, each student in Form 2 would have to make a choice between Home Economics and Pre-Technical Skills (Included in the B.D.T) as their final vocational subject choice for their final national exams (B.E.C.E.)


    The Basic Education Certificate Examination (B.E.C.E.) conducted by the West African Examination Council (W.A.E.C.), is the final national exam that our students write in order to graduate to Senior High Schools in Ghana. Thankfully to God, our past students have written and passed this exam with flying colours since 2006, with over 80% of them achieving Distinctions, a feat that we do not take for granted, and the school strives to improve year after year.


    On Friday mornings, we set aside a time of worship to our Maker and Almighty Father in Heaven, and engage the students in Bible exhortations and studies, to help strengthen their faith and morals. In the afternoons, they engage in club activities and sports like football, basketball, volleyball, table tennis and indoor games. Our school sports teams are hardworking and energetic, and have been making us proud in both internal and external tournaments.
